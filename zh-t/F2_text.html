A leaf of a fern looks almost exactly like the fern itself - just smaller. This observation may be used to create computer generated pictures of ferns (and also of other plants).

As in the previous visualization we have a copying machine that can create four smaller copies of an image. However in contrast to the previous applet we here want to create a very fine and granular picture of a fern. In order to achieve this we start with a single point and now randomly choose one of the copying machines to map the point. The mapped point is drawn and again taken as a starting point for a randomly chosen copying process. The mapped point is drawn again and chosen again as new starting point. This procedure is repeated several million times.

The simulation allows to adjust the position of the copy transformations, the colors and the probability distribution for the choice of the specific copying machines.
